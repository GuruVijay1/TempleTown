Sri Kokarneswarar Brahadambal Temple, Thirukokarnam, Pudukottai

November 16, 2016July 14, 2023 / TamHype / Leave a comment / Edit

The Sri Kokarneswarar and Araikasu Amman Brahadambal Temple, located in Thirukokarnam, Pudukottai, is a significant Hindu temple in the region. Here’s some information about the temple:

courtesy by pexels
courtesy by pexels
  1. History: The temple is believed to have been built during the Chola dynasty in the 11th century. It has undergone renovations and additions over the years by various rulers and devotees.
  2. Deities: The presiding deity of the temple is Lord Shiva, worshipped as Sri Kokarneswarar. The main sanctum houses the lingam, representing Lord Shiva. The temple also has a shrine dedicated to Araikasu Amman Brahadambal, the consort of Lord Kokarneswarar.
  3. Architecture: The temple showcases magnificent South Indian architectural style. The entrance features a towering gopuram (gateway tower) adorned with intricate sculptures and carvings. The temple complex consists of several mandapams (halls) and shrines.
  4. Festivals: The temple celebrates various festivals throughout the year, attracting a large number of devotees. The annual Brahmotsavam is a major festival, lasting for several days, during which the deities are taken out in a procession. Other important festivals include Shivaratri, Navaratri, and Arudra Darshanam.
  5. Religious Significance: The temple holds great spiritual significance for devotees of Lord Shiva and Goddess Brahadambal. It is considered a sacred site for worship, prayer, and seeking blessings.
  6. Location and Accessibility: The Sri Kokarneswarar and Araikasu Amman Brahadambal Temple is situated in Thirukokarnam, a village in Pudukottai district of Tamil Nadu, India. The temple can be accessed by road, and public transportation is available to reach the location.

Occupying an important place among the ancient classical monuments of Pudukkottai, the Thirugokarnam temple, popularly known as Brahadambal Temple, lies in the foot of a rock. This is one of the oldest temples in South India, with its history dating back to early 7th century AD and still in use.

Speciality of this temple:

This is a cave temple. There is a holy place for Araikasu Amman. If anyone is misplaced or lost anything, devotees pray for the recovery of the misplaced or lost things. It is believed that the misplaced or lost things will recovered.

Temple Times :
The temple is open from 6.00 a.m. to 11.00 a.m. and 4.00 p.m. to 8.00 p.m.
